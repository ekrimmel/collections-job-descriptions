# Natural History Collections & Biodiversity Informatics: Synthesized Job Classes

> Synthesized from 260 job postings drawn from freestanding natural history museums, university-based museums and herbaria, research institutes, government agencies, and informatics projects. Where relevant, equivalent US federal and state civil service classifications are noted. Salary figures are presented in US dollars and reflect the range observed across postings from approximately 2016–2026.

---

## 1. Collections Intern / Digitization Intern

### Equivalent Classifications
Not typically classified in federal/state civil service systems; where government-adjacent, may be associated with OPS or temporary workforce designations.

### Type of Employer
Freestanding natural history museums (AMNH, Denver Museum, California Botanic Garden, NHMLAC), university-based museums, and government-affiliated research institutions. Primarily grant-funded.

### Salary
$17–$25/hr; stipend-based positions range from approximately $3,650–$10,500 total for the internship term.

### Experience
None required; enrollment in an undergraduate or graduate program typical. Some internships require demonstrated interest or basic collections exposure.

### Education
Enrolled in or recent graduate of an undergraduate program in biology, botany, museum studies, earth sciences, or a related field.

---

### Description
Entry-level, time-limited positions designed to provide hands-on training in collections care, digitization, and data management. Interns work under close supervision on discrete projects — often grant-funded digitization initiatives — that contribute to making specimen records accessible online. These roles serve dual purposes as workforce development and collections improvement vehicles.

### Duties
- Mounting, barcoding, imaging, and filing herbarium or zoological specimens
- Transcribing specimen label data into collections databases
- Georeferencing specimen locality data
- Assisting with basic curation tasks (rehousing, pest management checks, inventory)
- Data cleanup and standardization in preparation for database migration
- Participating in public outreach events as available

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Enrollment in a relevant undergraduate or graduate program
- Attention to detail and patience with repetitive tasks
- Basic computer literacy (Microsoft Office, Google Suite)
- Ability to handle delicate specimens carefully
- Ability to work independently and as part of a team
- Strong organizational skills

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Prior experience in a herbarium or natural history museum setting
- Familiarity with collection databases (e.g., Symbiota, Arctos)
- Knowledge of world geography or plant/animal taxonomy
- Bilingual ability (Spanish or other languages a plus in many institutions)

### Physical Requirements
Ability to lift and carry boxes of specimens (20–30 lbs); climb ladders and step stools; stand or sit for extended periods at imaging stations; perform repetitive hand motions.

### Benefits
Generally limited or none for internships. Some stipend-based positions provide a fixed payment. Where affiliated with universities, interns may have access to campus resources.

## 2. Curatorial / Museum Technician (Entry-Level)

### Equivalent Classifications
- California State University: Instructional Support Technician II (Class Code 1617)
- SUNY: Instructional Support Specialist (SL4)

### Type of Employer
University-based museums, freestanding natural history museums, herbaria, and natural history survey institutions. Positions may be part-time, term-limited, or permanent hourly.

### Salary
$17–$35/hr; annualized full-time equivalents range from approximately $35,000–$65,000 depending on institution and location.

### Experience
0–2 years; often 1 year of collections or laboratory experience. Some positions accept relevant academic coursework as a substitute.

### Education
High school diploma minimum (some positions); Bachelor's degree in biology, botany, earth sciences, or museum studies typical; some positions prefer or require an associate's degree or higher.

---

### Description
Curatorial and museum technicians provide essential hands-on support for the physical care, documentation, and accessibility of natural history collections. They work under the direction of collection managers or curators, executing the daily tasks that keep collections organized, preserved, and scientifically useful. Titles across institutions include Curatorial Assistant I/II, Museum Technician, Museum Specialist, Specimen Digitizer, Herbarium Technician, and Vertebrate Technician. These are the working backbone of most active natural history collections.

### Duties
- Specimen preparation, mounting, labeling, and storage
- Cataloging and data entry into collections management systems
- Processing incoming and outgoing specimen loans
- Imaging specimens using digital photography and scanning equipment
- Georeferencing specimen locality data
- Integrated pest management (IPM) monitoring
- Environmental monitoring of collections storage areas
- Assisting visiting researchers and students
- Participating in collections reorganization and moves
- Supporting outreach events and guided tours

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Attention to detail and strong manual dexterity for handling delicate specimens
- Proficiency with Microsoft Office and standard computer applications
- Ability to follow established protocols and procedures
- Good written and verbal communication skills
- Ability to work independently and as part of a team
- Basic understanding of taxonomic principles (for biology-oriented positions)
- Familiarity with one or more collections management databases (e.g., Specify, Symbiota, EMu, Arctos)

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Bachelor's or associate's degree in a relevant biological or museum science field
- Prior museum or herbarium experience
- Experience with specimen preparation techniques (study skins, fluid preservation, mounting)
- Knowledge of taxonomy relevant to the collection type (botany, entomology, herpetology, etc.)
- Experience with digital imaging and image processing software
- Familiarity with collection databases and georeferencing tools

### Physical Requirements
Ability to lift 20–50 lbs; climb ladders and stairs; stand or remain stationary for extended periods; perform repetitive motions (data entry, mounting); reach above shoulder level; work with dust, chemical fumes (ethanol, formalin), and sharp instruments. Some positions require work with cryogenic materials (liquid nitrogen).

### Benefits
Variable. University-affiliated positions often offer comprehensive benefits (health, dental, retirement, tuition assistance, paid leave). Part-time or temporary positions typically offer limited or no benefits. State university positions may include CalPERS or comparable public employee retirement systems.

## 3. Archivist / Project Archivist

### Equivalent Classifications
No widely used federal/state civil service analog for natural history archives specifically; academic library systems may apply Librarian grade scales.

### Type of Employer
Freestanding natural history museums (AMNH) and university-based museums with substantial archival holdings. Often grant-funded (IMLS, NSF).

### Salary
$28–$33/hr for part-time; full-time equivalent approximately $55,000–$70,000.

### Experience
Minimum 2 years of professional archival processing experience, preferably in a museum or academic setting.

### Education
ALA-accredited Master's degree in library and information science (MLIS) or equivalent, with formal training in archival theory and practice. Enrollment in such a program also acceptable for some internship-level positions.

---

### Description
Archivists in natural history museum settings manage the documentary record associated with scientific collections — field notes, correspondence, photographic materials, maps, institutional records, and unpublished manuscripts. They process unorganized archival materials, create finding aids, perform basic preservation work, and provide access to researchers. Many positions are term-funded through federal grants (e.g., IMLS) and tied to specific collections improvement projects.

### Duties
- Assessing, analyzing, and arranging unprocessed archival materials
- Creating and updating catalog records and finding aids (often in ArchivesSpace using DACS/EAD/EAC standards)
- Performing basic preservation tasks (unfolding documents, rehousing in acid-free materials)
- Flagging materials for future conservation or digitization
- Responding to researcher inquiries
- Supervising interns or volunteers working in archival processing
- Maintaining project metrics and contributing to grant reporting
- Supporting social media outreach related to collections

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- ALA-accredited MLIS or equivalent with archival training
- At least 2 years of archival processing experience
- Demonstrated success in arranging and describing archival collections and writing finding aids
- Familiarity with basic preservation practices (handling, housing, storage)
- Proficiency in archival content management systems (e.g., ArchivesSpace)
- Knowledge of archival description standards (DACS, EAD, EAC)
- Ability to climb ladders and work with physically demanding collections (maps, flat files, oversize materials)
- Strong organizational skills and attention to detail

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Experience with scientific archives or museum archival collections
- Experience managing interns or volunteers
- Familiarity with digitization projects and current trends in archives and records management
- Comfort taking initiative in new settings

### Physical Requirements
Ability to climb ladders and stairs; lift and carry up to 30 lbs; work with delicate materials requiring careful handling.

### Benefits
Variable; university-affiliated or museum positions typically offer standard benefits packages.

## 4. Collections / Curatorial Associate (PhD-Level, Non-Tenure-Track)

### Equivalent Classifications
No standard federal classification; occasionally mapped to Research Scientist grades at universities.

### Type of Employer
Major freestanding natural history museums (AMNH, Harvard MCZ, Field Museum, NHMLA). These are non-tenure-track, PhD-required staff positions distinct from both collections managers and curatorial faculty.

### Salary
$80,000–$98,000/yr.

### Experience
Post-PhD experience expected; typically 2–7+ years of collections management and/or research experience depending on seniority.

### Education
Completed PhD or PhD candidate in the relevant scientific discipline (ichthyology, invertebrate zoology, malacology, herpetology, etc.).

---

### Description
The Curatorial Associate is a senior, PhD-level collections professional who leads and manages the daily scientific and operational work of a major natural history collection division. Reporting to a faculty curator-in-charge, the CA oversees staff, manages facilities and operations, coordinates fieldwork and acquisitions, administers databases and informatics programs, ensures regulatory compliance, seeks external funding, and serves as a liaison between curators and collections staff. This role is the operational hub of a collection division at large research museums.

### Duties
- Leading and managing day-to-day curation operations of a major collection division (often millions of specimens)
- Supervising museum specialists, curatorial assistants, volunteers, and interns
- Managing collection spaces, labs, facilities, supplies, and equipment
- Directing database and informatics programs; ensuring data integrity and public discoverability
- Overseeing incoming and outgoing loans, acquisitions, fieldwork, and permitting
- Ensuring compliance with federal and international wildlife regulations (CITES, USFWS, Nagoya Protocol)
- Working with curators and development staff to seek external funding
- Conducting annual collections reporting and other administrative directives
- Serving as departmental safety liaison and emergency warden

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Completed PhD or PhD candidacy in the relevant scientific discipline
- 2+ years of experience in collections management and care, including handling, rehousing, conservation, and data management
- Experience with specimen databases and digitization workflows
- Proficiency with collections management systems (e.g., KE EMu, Arctos, Specify)
- Ability to supervise and develop a diverse team
- Strong written, verbal, and interpersonal communication skills
- Knowledge of relevant regulatory frameworks (federal permitting, wildlife transactions)

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Knowledge of invertebrate, vertebrate, or other relevant taxonomic groups
- Experience managing type material and/or working with public data repositories
- Familiarity with data sharing standards and aggregators (iDigBio, GBIF)

### Physical Requirements
Ability to remain stationary for extended periods; occasional lifting up to 20–50 lbs; move about museum campus; climb and balance on physical structures as needed.

### Benefits
Comprehensive institutional benefits packages, typically including medical, dental, vision, life insurance, and retirement plans. AMNH positions are explicitly noted as offering "extensive benefits."

## 5. Collections Manager

### Equivalent Classifications
- Florida Museum of Natural History (UF): FLMNH Collections Manager II / III
- SUNY ESF: Instructional Support Specialist (SL4)
- Washington State University: Preservation & Museum Specialist 2/3/4
- University of Wisconsin: Collections Manager II (LM017)
- University of Alaska: Library Professional 3, Grade 81
- NC Museum of Natural Sciences: Natural Science Research Curator I (Grade NC09)
- California State University: Instructional Support Technician II

### Type of Employer
University-based natural history museums, freestanding natural history museums, state natural history surveys, government agencies, botanical gardens, and herbaria. The most common professional-level position in the dataset.

### Salary
Broad range: approximately $32,000–$84,000/yr depending on institution type, geographic location, and seniority. Mid-range is roughly $45,000–$65,000 for full-time positions at universities; freestanding museums and larger institutions often pay $55,000–$78,000+.

### Experience
Typically 2–5 years for entry to mid-level; 5+ years for senior-level positions. Some positions accept graduate degrees as partial substitutes for experience.

### Education
Bachelor's degree minimum in biology, botany, earth sciences, zoology, or museum studies. Master's degree strongly preferred and often required. PhD preferred for research-intensive positions at major institutions.

---

### Description
The Collections Manager is the professional steward of a natural history collection or collection division. They are responsible for the physical care, preservation, organization, and growth of specimens; the management of associated data and databases; the facilitation of research and educational access; and the supervision of support staff, students, and volunteers. Collections managers work closely with faculty curators and researchers, often co-authoring grants and contributing to publications, but the primary orientation of the role is collections stewardship rather than independent research. Positions span taxonomic domains (vertebrate zoology, invertebrate zoology, paleontology, botany, entomology, mineralogy, anthropology) and institution types.

### Duties
- Daily curation: accessioning, cataloging, labeling, housing, organizing, and conserving specimens
- Database management and digitization: maintaining and improving specimen records in collections management systems; contributing to georeferencing, imaging, and data publication workflows
- Loan management: processing incoming and outgoing loans; ensuring compliance with state, federal, and international regulations (CITES, USFWS, APHIS, Nagoya Protocol); maintaining associated documentation and permits
- Environmental monitoring: conducting integrated pest management (IPM), monitoring temperature and humidity, and managing the dermestid colony (where applicable)
- Fieldwork: collecting specimens to expand collection holdings as approved
- Supervision: training and directing student workers, interns, and volunteers
- Grant writing: assisting curators in developing proposals for collection improvement funding
- Outreach: providing tours, supporting exhibits, responding to public and researcher inquiries
- Reporting: tracking collection metrics, compiling annual reports

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Knowledge of accepted museum standards and practices for collection care, documentation, and conservation
- Experience with collection management databases (Specify, Arctos, EMu, Symbiota, or equivalents)
- Familiarity with taxonomic nomenclature relevant to the collection
- Understanding of IPM protocols
- Knowledge of relevant regulatory requirements for specimen acquisition, shipping, and curation
- Strong organizational skills and attention to detail
- Ability to work independently and supervise others
- Effective written and oral communication

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Master's or doctoral degree in a relevant field
- Specimen preparation experience (study skins, fluid specimens, skeletal preparations, herbarium mounting, fossil preparation)
- Familiarity with biodiversity data aggregators (GBIF, iDigBio, VertNet, etc.)
- Experience with grant proposal development
- Georeferencing and GIS skills
- Experience with digital imaging and photographic equipment

### Physical Requirements
Frequent lifting (typically 25–50 lbs); climbing ladders and stairs; bending, stooping, and kneeling; standing and walking for extended periods; exposure to preservation chemicals (ethanol, formalin, pesticide residues on older specimens), dust, mold, and sharp instruments; occasional fieldwork in varied outdoor conditions.

### Benefits
Comprehensive benefits at most full-time positions: health/dental/vision insurance, retirement plans (TIAA, PERS, TRS, or equivalent), paid leave, tuition benefits (at universities), professional development support. Some positions include an RTD eco pass (Denver), transit passes, or free museum membership.

## 6. Herbarium Curator / Collections Manager (Botany-Specific)

### Equivalent Classifications
- Smithsonian NMNH: Museum Specialist and Technician, Series 1016, GS-13 (for senior supervisory positions)
- University of Wisconsin: Collections Manager II (LM017)
- Cal Poly Humboldt: Instructional Support Technician II

### Type of Employer
University herbaria (the dominant employer type in this category), botanical gardens (Missouri Botanical Garden, California Botanic Garden, Morton Arboretum, Santa Barbara Botanic Garden), and freestanding natural history museums with botany divisions (Field Museum, NHMLA, Carnegie Museum).

### Salary
$39,400–$95,000/yr; higher end reflects senior supervisory roles at major institutions. Typical range for a fully professional position is $45,000–$75,000.

### Experience
1–5 years of herbarium curatorial experience typical; senior and supervisory positions require 5+ years. Some positions with a PhD accept reduced experience.

### Education
Bachelor's degree in botany, plant sciences, biology, or related field (minimum). Master's degree strongly preferred. PhD required or preferred for research-active positions and larger herbaria.

---

### Description
Herbarium Collections Managers and Herbarium Curators oversee the care, development, and accessibility of pressed plant (and fungal) specimen collections, which may range from tens of thousands to millions of specimens. In addition to the core collections management functions shared with other disciplines, herbarium positions often emphasize botanical taxonomic expertise, proficiency with herbarium-specific databases and portals (Symbiota, CCH, SEINet), and a working knowledge of plant systematics. Many positions include outreach and educational roles, fieldwork for specimen collection, and involvement in regional botanical initiatives.

### Duties
- Specimen mounting, repair, and conservation
- Taxonomic identification, annotation, and nomenclatural updating
- Loan processing and exchange management
- Database management, digitization, and georeferencing
- Supervising volunteers, interns, and student workers
- Grant writing and management
- Public outreach and educational tours
- Fieldwork and specimen collection
- Liaison with regional herbarium networks and data portals

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Knowledge of plant systematics and herbarium procedures (mounting, labeling, pest management, loan protocols)
- Proficiency with herbarium databases (Symbiota, Specify, or equivalent) and herbarium portals (CCH, SEINet, GBIF, iDigBio)
- Understanding of plant taxonomy and nomenclature
- Experience with large-scale specimen processing
- Strong organizational skills and attention to detail
- Effective communication and interpersonal skills

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- PhD in botany, plant systematics, or related field
- Knowledge of regional flora (California, intermountain West, etc.)
- Familiarity with georeferencing tools and GIS
- Grant writing experience
- Supervisory experience with staff or volunteers
- Multilingual ability (Spanish, French, Latin for literature access)

### Physical Requirements
Lifting and carrying 20–40 lbs; climbing rolling ladders and step stools; standing for extended periods; occasional fieldwork including hiking on uneven terrain, exposure to inclement weather, and plant collection in remote settings.

### Benefits
Comprehensive benefits at university and botanical garden positions. Some institutions (BYU, FHSU, Utah Valley University) offer exceptional retirement matching, tuition waivers, transit passes, and wellness programs.

## 7. Digitization Manager / Digitization Supervisor

### Equivalent Classifications
No standard civil service classification; sometimes mapped to research or program specialist grades at universities.

### Type of Employer
University herbaria and museums (Harvard, Missouri Botanical Garden), freestanding natural history museums, and botanical gardens. Typically grant-funded (NSF, IMLS).

### Salary
$60,000–$120,000/yr depending on seniority and institution. Mid-level managers typically $65,000–$90,000.

### Experience
3–5+ years of experience in digitization, preferably in museum/herbarium contexts; 2+ years of supervisory experience for manager-level positions.

### Education
Master's degree in biology, museum studies, library/information science, or related field preferred. Bachelor's degree with substantial experience accepted.

---

### Description
Digitization Managers and Supervisors lead teams engaged in the systematic conversion of physical natural history collections into digital records and images accessible through online portals. They develop and document workflows, ensure data quality and consistency, manage imaging equipment and software, supervise digitizers and volunteers, and coordinate with informatics staff to move data through processing pipelines to aggregators such as iDigBio and GBIF. These roles are heavily project-management oriented and require both technical knowledge of digitization processes and strong people management skills.

### Duties
- Developing, testing, documenting, and improving digitization workflows
- Supervising and training digitization technicians, interns, and volunteers
- Ensuring data quality control across imaging, transcription, and georeferencing
- Managing imaging equipment (cameras, conveyor systems, scanners) and troubleshooting
- Coordinating data export and submission to external aggregators and portals
- Tracking project progress and preparing reports for funders
- Collaborating with collections staff, IT, and external contractors
- Participating in consortium meetings and professional working groups

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Advanced knowledge of digital photography (color standardization, metadata capture, archival storage)
- Proficiency with image processing software (Capture One, Adobe Photoshop)
- Experience with collections database systems (Specify, EMu, Symbiota)
- Strong project management and organizational skills
- Supervisory experience
- Effective written and verbal communication

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Knowledge of biodiversity data standards (Darwin Core)
- Familiarity with project management software
- Knowledge of botanical terminology (for herbarium contexts)
- Experience with workflow automation

### Physical Requirements
Sitting for extended periods at computer workstations; occasional lifting up to 20 lbs; may require work at varying heights (ladders, step stools); requires clarity of vision including color perception.

### Benefits
Variable; grant-funded positions often temporary with standard institutional benefits where applicable.

## 8. Biodiversity Informatics Specialist / Database Developer

### Equivalent Classifications
- NC Museum of Natural Sciences: User Support Specialist (Grade DT07)
- Indiana Geological & Water Survey: PAE-3IT
- UC: various Academic title series

### Type of Employer
University-based biocollections centers (ASU BioKIC, iDigBio at UF, Illinois Natural History Survey, Yale Peabody), natural history museums with large informatics programs (NHMLA, Field Museum, Academy of Natural Sciences), government survey institutions, and international organizations (BGCI, UNESCO OBIS).

### Salary
$48,000–$100,000/yr; wide range reflects significant variation between entry-level informatics positions and senior database architects or developer roles. Mid-level positions cluster around $55,000–$80,000.

### Experience
2–5 years of database development or biodiversity informatics experience typical. Senior roles require 5–10+ years.

### Education
Bachelor's degree in computer science, information technology, biology, or a related field; equivalent experience accepted. Master's or PhD preferred for research-oriented or senior positions.

---

### Description
Biodiversity informatics specialists design, build, maintain, and support the digital infrastructure that makes natural history collections data accessible to researchers and the public. The field spans a wide spectrum from database administration and software development to portal management, data standards compliance, and user support. Many positions are tied to specific platforms (Symbiota, EMu, Arctos, Specify, GBIF IPT) or large-scale digitization networks (iDigBio, TCNs). Job titles include Database Developer/Programmer, Biodiversity Data Manager, Collections Database Administrator, Data Systems Analyst, Biodiversity Informatician, and Software Engineer.

### Duties
- Designing, building, and maintaining relational databases (MySQL, PostgreSQL, MariaDB)
- Developing web-based data entry and access interfaces
- Ensuring data integrity, quality, and compliance with biodiversity data standards (Darwin Core, EML)
- Providing technical support and training to collections staff and external users
- Coordinating data publication and sharing with aggregators (GBIF, iDigBio)
- Developing and maintaining data pipelines, import/export workflows, and reporting tools
- Managing server infrastructure and database environments
- Writing documentation, standard procedures, and user guides
- Collaborating with curators, collections managers, and researchers to understand and address data needs
- Contributing to grant proposals for informatics infrastructure

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Proficiency in SQL and relational database design (MySQL, MariaDB, or PostgreSQL)
- Experience with at least one scripting or programming language (Python, PHP, JavaScript, R)
- Familiarity with web application development (HTML, CSS, backend frameworks)
- Knowledge of biodiversity data standards (Darwin Core) and common platforms (Symbiota, Specify, EMu, or Arctos)
- Effective communication skills for working with non-technical users
- Strong attention to detail and analytical problem-solving skills

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Background in zoology, botany, or ecology (helps translate between science and technology)
- Experience with specific platforms: Symbiota, Axiell EMu, Specify, Arctos
- Familiarity with LAMP stack (Linux, Apache, MySQL, PHP) environment
- Experience with data aggregators (iDigBio, GBIF) and publication tools (IPT)
- Knowledge of georeferencing, GIS, or spatial data
- Experience with Digital Asset Management Systems
- Ability to work with version control (Git/GitHub)

### Physical Requirements
Primarily sedentary; standard office or laboratory environment; extended periods at a computer.

### Benefits
University positions typically offer full benefits packages including retirement matching, health insurance, tuition assistance, and transit subsidies. Government positions offer comparable public employee benefits. Remote or hybrid work is explicitly offered by some institutions (ASU, Drexel, Wisconsin).

## 9. Collections Manager / Curator (Researcher-Track)

*Distinct from the standard Collections Manager role in that research and publication are explicit, significant components (10–25% of time or more); these positions may lead to or overlap with curatorial appointments.*

### Equivalent Classifications
- Florida Museum of Natural History: FLMNH Collections Manager II / III (senior levels)
- Illinois Natural History Survey: Assistant Research Scientist / Associate Research Scientist
- Virginia Museum of Natural History: Scientist II, Pay Band 05
- Smithsonian NMNH: Museum Specialist Series 1016, GS-13 (supervisory)

### Type of Employer
Major university natural history museums (Field Museum, Florida Museum, University of Michigan UMMZ, University of Colorado, University of Kansas Biodiversity Institute), state natural history surveys (Illinois Natural History Survey), and state museums (Virginia Museum of Natural History, Royal British Columbia Museum).

### Salary
$45,000–$80,000+ depending on institution and seniority. Research-active positions at major institutions often range $55,000–$75,000 for the collections management component.

### Experience
5–10+ years of collections experience; post-doctoral experience preferred or required for research scientist titles.

### Education
Master's degree minimum; PhD strongly preferred or required. Active research record expected.

---

### Description
These positions occupy a hybrid space between traditional collections management and curatorial research. The incumbent is expected to conduct and publish independent or collaborative research using the collection, in addition to managing its daily care and use. Research time is often formally allocated (10–25%). They may supervise graduate students and contribute to graduate education programs. Some positions carry adjunct faculty appointments. The expectation of external funding through grants is common. This class is most prevalent at well-resourced university museums and state natural history surveys.

### Duties
*(All duties of the Collections Manager role, plus:)*
- Conducting independent or collaborative specimen-based research
- Publishing findings in peer-reviewed journals
- Presenting at professional meetings
- Developing and submitting external grant proposals as PI or co-PI
- Supervising and mentoring graduate students
- Teaching museum-related courses (mammalogy, collections management, etc.)
- Serving on graduate student committees

### Required Skills and Qualifications
*(All qualifications of the Collections Manager role, plus:)*
- PhD in biology, zoology, geology, botany, or related field
- Active research agenda in relevant taxonomic or methodological area
- Demonstrated record of or clear potential for peer-reviewed publication
- Grant-writing experience or training

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Post-doctoral experience
- Record of securing external funding
- Experience with advanced specimen analysis techniques (molecular methods, CT scanning, morphometrics)
- Fieldwork experience, including international field collection

### Physical Requirements
Standard collections environment (see Collections Manager); fieldwork may require strenuous outdoor activity in adverse conditions, overnight travel, and work with wildlife.

### Benefits
Full institutional benefits; research positions at universities often include formal professional development support and access to computing resources.

## 10. Senior Collections Manager / Director of Collections

### Equivalent Classifications
- Smithsonian NMNH: Museum Specialist Series 1016, GS-13 (supervisory, with supervisory differential)

### Type of Employer
Freestanding natural history museums (Carnegie Museum, Denver Museum of Nature & Science, Field Museum, NHMLA), university-based museums with large multi-division collections (AMNH, Florida Museum, University of Michigan), and national institutions (Smithsonian).

### Salary
$80,000–$130,000/yr. The Carnegie CMNH Director of Collections Care & Access position was posted at $100,000–$120,000. Smithsonian GS-13 equivalent runs $121,785–$158,322.

### Experience
10–15+ years of progressively responsible experience in museum collections care; significant management and budget experience required.

### Education
Bachelor's degree minimum; advanced degree (Master's or PhD) strongly preferred. Some senior roles effectively require a PhD and a research background to engage credibly with scientific staff.

---

### Description
Senior collections leaders provide strategic and operational oversight of entire collections departments or divisions spanning multiple collecting areas. They manage large teams of collections professionals, develop long-term collections strategies, oversee facilities, lead accreditation processes, secure and administer major grants, ensure legal and ethical compliance across all collections activities, and serve as senior advocates for collections care within the institution. These are executive-level positions with significant budget and personnel authority. Titles include Director of Collections Care & Access, VP of Collections and Research, Head of Collections Services, and Supervisory Museum Specialist.

### Duties
- Setting strategic vision and long-term goals for institutional collections
- Supervising and developing a multi-professional collections staff across multiple disciplines
- Overseeing facilities planning, pest management, environmental monitoring, and emergency preparedness
- Managing divisional budgets and developing fiscal plans
- Leading institutional accreditation processes (AAM)
- Ensuring consistent collections policies, documentation, and legal compliance across all units
- Working with development staff to secure sponsored and contributed revenues
- Partnering with IT and informatics teams on digital infrastructure strategy
- Representing the institution in national and international collections communities
- Supporting exhibition development, education programs, and donor engagement

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- 10–15+ years of museum experience with progressive leadership responsibilities
- Sophisticated understanding of collections management standards, ethics, and professional norms across multiple disciplines
- Proven ability to build consensus across diverse stakeholders
- Exceptional communication, change management, and supervisory skills
- Strong financial management and budget development experience
- History of grant acquisition and management
- Deep knowledge of digital technologies and standards relevant to collections databases

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- PhD in a relevant field
- Experience in collections-related decolonization, NAGPRA compliance, or repatriation processes
- Understanding of library and archive collections in addition to specimen collections

### Physical Requirements
Primarily administrative and sedentary; occasional physical demands including climbing ladders, lifting up to 25 lbs, overseeing specimen handling and condition reporting in galleries and storage areas.

### Benefits
Comprehensive executive-level benefits. Smithsonian GS-13 positions include federal employee health, life, and accident insurance; an excellent retirement program; and flexible spending accounts.

## 11. Curator / Assistant Curator (Tenure-Track or Research-Primary)

### Equivalent Classifications
- University of Florida (VMNH): Scientist II, Pay Band 05
- University-based positions: Faculty title series (Assistant/Associate/Full Professor with Museum appointment)

### Type of Employer
University-based natural history museums where curators hold tenure-track or equivalent research faculty appointments (Florida Museum, Sam Noble Museum, Virginia Museum of Natural History, MVZ Berkeley, Illinois Natural History Survey at research scientist levels). Freestanding museums occasionally offer curator positions with significant research components (Bishop Museum, Santa Barbara Museum of Natural History, Royal British Columbia Museum).

### Salary
Typically competitive and negotiable; ranges from approximately $55,000 (state museum entry-level Scientist II) to well above $100,000 for senior faculty-equivalent positions at major research universities. Salaries are often not disclosed in postings.

### Experience
Post-doctoral research experience strongly preferred; demonstrated record of publications and external funding.

### Education

### Description
Curators at this level are primarily researchers who use natural history collections as their scientific resource. They typically supervise a collections manager or team of specialists who handle the day-to-day operational care of the collection. ### Duties

### Desired Skills and Qualifications

- Experience with exhibit development and public communication
- Record of collaborations within and outside the home institution

### Physical Requirements
Variable; may include strenuous fieldwork in remote settings, laboratory research environments, and standard office conditions.

Full faculty or research scientist benefits, typically including comprehensive health coverage, institutional retirement contributions, sabbatical eligibility, and professional development support.
