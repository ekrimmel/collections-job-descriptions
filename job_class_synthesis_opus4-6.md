

# Natural History Collections Job Classes: A Comprehensive Career Reference

## Introduction

This document synthesizes data from approximately 200 job postings spanning 2016–2026, drawn from natural history museums, university herbaria and zoological museums, botanical gardens, government agencies, and related institutions across the United States and internationally. The postings have been analyzed and organized into **16 distinct job classes** that represent the major career tracks available in the natural history collections field.

Several overarching patterns emerge from the data:

- **Salary compression** is pronounced across collections management roles: positions requiring a PhD and 5+ years of experience at smaller institutions may pay less than entry-level positions at major museums in high-cost-of-living areas.
- **The Collections Manager role** is by far the most frequently posted position and shows the widest variation in scope, salary, and requirements across institution types.
- **Biodiversity informatics** is a rapidly growing specialization, with positions spanning from database administration to software engineering to data science leadership.
- **Term/grant-funded positions** are common, particularly for digitization-focused roles and assistant-level positions, creating career instability in the field.
- **Employer type** strongly predicts salary ranges, benefits quality, and position stability — federal (Smithsonian), large freestanding museums (AMNH, Field Museum), and R1 universities generally offer the best compensation.
- **Physical requirements** are near-universal: most positions require lifting 20–50 lbs, climbing ladders, standing for prolonged periods, and working with chemicals.

## 1. Collections Manager

### Equivalent Classifications
- GS-9 through GS-12 (federal, depending on scope)
- State classifications vary: NC "Natural Science Research Curator I" (NC09); California "Instructional Support Technician II"
- University titles: "Curator" (Auburn, Texas A&M), "Research Specialist" (ASU), "Collections Manager" (most institutions)

### Type of Employer
Found at virtually every type of institution: freestanding natural history museums (NHMLA, Field Museum, DMNS, Carnegie), university museums (Cornell, CU Boulder, University of Michigan, LSU, SNOMNH), state museums (NC Museum of Natural Sciences, Royal BC Museum), botanical gardens (California Botanic Garden, Missouri Botanical Garden), and smaller regional museums (Sternberg, Pioneer Trails). This is the most common and broadly distributed role in the field.

### Salary
- **Range:** $29,000–$84,000/year USD
- Small university museums: $31,300–$45,000 (Angelo State, Auburn, SNOMNH at $18.27/hr ≈ $38,000)
- Mid-size institutions: $40,000–$63,000 (DMNS at $58,000–$63,000; Cleveland at $40,000–$45,000; Cornell at $21.90–$24.60/hr ≈ $45,500–$51,200)
- Large museums and major universities: $55,000–$84,000 (Bell Museum at $70,000–$78,000; CU Boulder at $56,000–$84,000; University of Michigan at $48,000–$67,700; Field Museum at $61,000–$74,000)
- Canadian positions: $58,630–$70,557 CAD (~$43,000–$52,000 USD) at Royal BC Museum
- **Drivers of variation:** Institution size and prestige, geographic cost of living, collection complexity and size, supervisory responsibilities, and whether the position includes a research component.

### Experience
- **Minimum:** 0–2 years (entry-level at smaller institutions or with advanced degree)
- **Typical:** 2–5 years of collections or museum experience
- **Senior positions:** 5–8+ years, including supervisory experience (Bell Museum, Field Museum, NHMLA)
- Tiered systems exist at some institutions (Auburn: Level I/II/III corresponding to 0/2/4 years)

### Education
- **Minimum:** Bachelor's degree in biology, zoology, botany, geology, museum studies, or related field
- **Typical:** Master's degree in the relevant discipline (many positions list this as required or strongly preferred)
- **Senior positions:** PhD preferred or required at major research institutions (Field Museum, University of Michigan)
- At some institutions, extensive experience can substitute for formal education

---

### Description
The Collections Manager is the backbone of natural history collections operations. This role is responsible for the day-to-day care, preservation, organization, documentation, and accessibility of a museum's natural history specimens and associated data. Collections Managers serve as the primary stewards of physical collections, ensuring specimens are properly housed, documented, and available for research, education, and exhibition. They typically report to a curator or museum director and work closely with researchers, students, volunteers, and the public. The scope ranges from managing a single disciplinary collection at a small institution to overseeing multiple sub-collections at a large museum.

### Duties
- Manage day-to-day curatorial operations including specimen preparation, identification, cataloging, housing, labeling, organization, and conservation
- Process and manage incoming and outgoing specimen loans, exchanges, gifts, and accessions, including compliance with CITES, USFWS, APHIS, IATA, and other regulatory frameworks
- Maintain and develop collections databases (Specify, Arctos, EMu, Symbiota, MCZbase, etc.) including data entry, quality control, and data publication to aggregators (GBIF, iDigBio, VertNet)
- Coordinate and implement digitization projects including specimen imaging, georeferencing, and metadata capture
- Implement and maintain integrated pest management (IPM) programs and environmental monitoring (temperature, humidity)
- Manage specimen preparation facilities (dermestid colonies, wet labs, freezers, cryogenic storage)
- Recruit, train, and supervise students (graduate and undergraduate), volunteers, interns, and in some cases professional staff
- Manage state, federal, and international permits for collecting, holding, and transporting specimens
- Process visitor requests, facilitate research access, and host visiting scientists
- Write or assist with grant proposals for collections improvement and manage grant-funded projects
- Prepare annual reports on collection activities, growth, and use
- Manage section/division budgets, order supplies and equipment
- Participate in public outreach, education programs, exhibitions, and tours
- Develop, update, and implement collections policies and standard operating procedures
- Participate in emergency preparedness planning for collections
- May conduct limited independent or collaborative research (typically 10–20% time at research institutions)
- Participate in professional development and professional societies (SPNHC, disciplinary societies)
- Coordinate with other museum departments (exhibits, education, development, security, facilities)

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Knowledge of taxonomic principles, nomenclature, and systematics relevant to the collection discipline
- Proficiency with collections management database software (Specify, Arctos, EMu, Symbiota, or similar)
- Knowledge of museum best practices for collections care, conservation, and preservation
- Specimen preparation skills appropriate to the discipline (study skins, fluid preservation, mounting, skeletal preparation, etc.)
- Strong organizational skills and meticulous attention to detail
- Ability to work independently and collaboratively
- Excellent written and verbal communication skills
- Computer proficiency including Microsoft Office and database software
- Knowledge of relevant regulatory frameworks (CITES, Endangered Species Act, Nagoya Protocol, etc.)
- Supervisory and mentoring ability

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Experience with digitization workflows, imaging, and georeferencing
- Knowledge of biodiversity data standards (Darwin Core)
- Experience with data aggregators and portals (GBIF, iDigBio, MorphoSource)
- Grant writing experience
- Field collection experience
- Programming/scripting skills (R, Python, SQL)
- Knowledge of regional flora/fauna
- Experience with 3D imaging or CT scanning
- Public speaking and science communication skills
- Familiarity with GIS software

### Physical Requirements
- Lifting 25–50 lbs regularly; occasionally up to 100 lbs with assistance
- Climbing ladders and stairs; reaching above shoulder level
- Standing, walking, bending, stooping, kneeling, and crouching for extended periods
- Fine manual dexterity for specimen handling
- Working with chemicals (ethanol, formalin, pesticides, liquid nitrogen)
- Exposure to dust, mold, allergens, and occasionally hazardous legacy materials
- Occasional fieldwork in variable weather conditions

### Benefits
- Health, dental, and vision insurance (typically comprehensive at large institutions)
- Retirement plans (403(b), state pension systems, TIAA-CREF)
- Paid vacation and sick leave (varies widely; 2–4 weeks typical)
- Professional development opportunities and conference attendance
- Opportunity for limited research (up to 20% at some institutions)
- Tuition benefits at university-affiliated positions
- Free or discounted museum membership

## 2. Museum Specialist / Curatorial Assistant

### Equivalent Classifications
- GS-5 through GS-9 (federal)
- "Museum Specialist" (AMNH), "Curatorial Assistant I/II/III" (California Academy of Sciences, Harvard MCZ, Carnegie), "Museum Technician" (Amherst)

### Type of Employer
Primarily found at large freestanding natural history museums (AMNH, California Academy of Sciences, Harvard MCZ) and some mid-size institutions. This is the entry-to-mid-level professional collections position at institutions large enough to maintain separate specialist and manager tracks.

### Salary
- **Range:** $24.00/hr–$60,000/year ($42,970–$59,946 most common at AMNH; $29.00–$35.78/hr at California Academy of Sciences)
- Part-time positions as low as $17.00–$24.00/hr (California Botanic Garden, Amherst)
- Harvard MCZ: Grade 053 ($50,122–$78,291)
- **Drivers of variation:** Institution, geographic location, experience level, and whether the position is full-time or part-time

### Experience
- **Range:** 0–6 years
- Entry-level (Museum Specialist / Curatorial Assistant I): Often no formal experience required beyond degree
- Mid-level (Curatorial Assistant II/III): 2–3+ years museum collections experience
- Senior (Collections Specialist, Senior at Cal Academy): 6+ years including supervisory experience

### Education
- **Minimum:** Bachelor's degree in biology, zoology, botany, earth science, or related field (some positions accept high school diploma with experience)
- **Mid-level:** Bachelor's required; Master's preferred
- **Senior specialist:** Master's often required or equivalent experience

---

### Description
Museum Specialists and Curatorial Assistants are the hands-on practitioners of collections work. These positions exist primarily at institutions large enough to have a tiered staffing structure where they work under the direction of a Collections Manager and/or Curatorial Associate. They focus on the physical care and documentation of specimens, including preparation, cataloging, data entry, loan processing, and assisting researchers. The role provides critical daily operational support and is often the entry point for a career in collections.

### Duties
- Specimen preparation, identification, housing, labeling, organizing, and shelving, including major collections moves
- Collections cataloging, including accessions/deaccessions, digitization, and database management
- Environmental monitoring of storage facilities and laboratories; pest management
- Monitoring preservation/condition of objects and integrity of storage systems
- Chemical safety, inventory control, lab maintenance, equipment and supplies management
- Specimen retrieval and housing; packing/unpacking and shipping
- Processing information requests and managing curatorial approval processes
- Coordinating visits and assisting visiting researchers
- Specimen photography and digital imaging
- Data entry, verification, and quality control in collections databases
- Processing incoming and outgoing loans
- Assisting with fieldwork and specimen preparation
- Training volunteers and interns in basic collections tasks
- Participating in collections-related fieldwork, workshops, and conferences
- Wildlife import/export declarations and permitting as applicable

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Bachelor's degree in relevant biological or earth science field
- Good organizational skills and attention to detail
- Ability to work independently and as part of a team
- Manual dexterity sufficient for handling delicate specimens
- Proficiency with Microsoft Office (Word, Excel)
- Basic familiarity with collections databases
- Good written and verbal communication skills

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Previous museum or collections experience
- Knowledge of relevant taxonomy and systematics
- Experience with databases (EMu, Specify, Arctos, Symbiota)
- Digital photography and imaging skills
- Georeferencing experience
- Knowledge of relevant regulatory frameworks

### Physical Requirements
- Lifting 20–50 lbs; occasionally more with assistance
- Climbing ladders and stairs
- Standing and sitting for prolonged periods
- Fine manual dexterity for specimen handling
- Working with preservation chemicals (ethanol, formalin)
- Exposure to dust, mold, and allergens

### Benefits
- Full benefits packages at most full-time positions (health, dental, vision, retirement)
- Overtime eligibility common at AMNH and similar institutions
- Professional development opportunities
- Part-time positions typically do not include benefits

## 3. Curatorial Associate

### Equivalent Classifications
- GS-12 through GS-13 (federal equivalent)
- "Curatorial Associate" (AMNH, Harvard MCZ)

### Type of Employer
Large freestanding natural history museums (AMNH, Harvard MCZ) and some major university museums. This is a senior, PhD-level collections leadership position distinct from both the research curator and the collections manager tracks.

### Salary
- **Range:** $80,000–$98,000/year
- AMNH Ichthyology: $80,000–$86,000
- AMNH Invertebrate Zoology: $93,000–$98,000
- Harvard MCZ Malacology: Not specified but typically comparable

### Experience
- **Minimum:** 2 years of collections management experience and 2 years working with specimen databases (AMNH IZ)
- **Typical:** 7+ years of curatorial experience including supervisory experience (Harvard MCZ Malacology)
- **Research experience** in the relevant taxonomic group is typically required

### Education
- PhD or PhD candidacy in the relevant zoological/botanical discipline (required)
- Master's degree minimum at Harvard MCZ

---

### Description
The Curatorial Associate is a senior, non-tenure-track professional position that serves as the operational leader of a collections division. This role bridges the gap between research curators (who focus primarily on scholarship) and collections management staff. The Curatorial Associate typically has PhD-level expertise in the relevant discipline and is responsible for leading and managing support staff, overseeing all aspects of collections curation, setting priorities for collections improvement, managing the informatics program, and working with curators to seek external funding. This position is found primarily at the largest natural history museums.

### Duties
- Lead and manage the division's collections curation program
- Supervise and collaborate with Museum Specialists; assign tasks, manage effort, monitor performance
- Manage use and upkeep of departmental spaces, labs, facilities, supplies, and equipment
- Direct collections management operations; develop short- and long-range objectives
- Maintain collections informatics programs, databases, data integrity, and security
- Oversee annual collections reporting and administrative directives
- Manage incoming/outgoing loan activities, shipping/receiving, permitting, and compliance
- Work with curators and development office to acquire external funding
- Train staff and volunteers on operations and equipment
- Manage the department website
- Serve as departmental safety liaison
- Manage visitation to collections, tours, and outreach activities
- Determine short- and long-term collection priorities
- Assist with grant proposal preparation and supervise funded projects
- Oversee import/export permit processes and regulatory compliance
- Develop policies and processes that conform to best-practice standards (SPNHC, AAM)

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- PhD or PhD candidacy in relevant discipline
- Multiple years of experience in collections management and care
- Proficiency with collections databases (EMu, MCZbase, or similar)
- Experience working with specimen databases and digitization workflows
- Knowledge of federal and state permitting requirements
- Demonstrated writing, organizational, and supervisory skills
- Excellent interpersonal and communication skills

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Knowledge of specific taxonomic groups relevant to the position
- Experience managing type material
- Proficiency with data sharing standards and public repositories
- Grant writing experience
- Experience with digital imaging technologies

### Physical Requirements
- Standing for extended periods; walking and climbing
- Lifting and moving objects up to 50 lbs
- Climbing ladders and stairs
- Working with preservation chemicals

### Benefits
- Comprehensive benefits packages at major institutions
- Professional development including attendance at national/international meetings
- Opportunity for limited research

## 4. Assistant Collections Manager

### Equivalent Classifications
- No standard civil service equivalent; typically internal institutional titles
- "Assistant Collections Manager" (DMNS, NHMLA, Stanford)

### Type of Employer
Found at larger museums that maintain a tiered collections staffing structure, including freestanding museums (DMNS, NHMLA) and university-affiliated programs (Stanford Libraries). These positions support the work of senior collections managers and are often grant-funded or term-limited.

### Salary
- **Range:** $22.00–$26.44/hr ($45,760–$55,000 annualized) at NHMLA; $23.00–$25.00/hr ($47,840–$52,000) at DMNS
- Stanford: $76,000–$101,000 (higher due to cost of living and librarian-track classification)
- **Drivers of variation:** Geographic location, institution type, and whether position is permanent or term-funded

### Experience
- **Minimum:** 1 year museum collections management and 1 year relational database experience (DMNS)
- **Range:** 1–3 years of collections experience
- Molecular genetics lab experience additionally required for marine biodiversity positions (NHMLA)

### Education
- **Minimum:** Bachelor's degree in relevant field
- **Preferred:** Master's degree
- Stanford requires advanced degree in museum studies, geology, or related field

---

### Description
Assistant Collections Managers provide direct support to senior collections management staff. They assist with the full range of collections management activities but typically focus on specific project areas such as digitization, rehousing, or particular taxonomic sub-collections. These positions often serve as career development roles for individuals building toward collections manager positions. Many are grant-funded with defined terms.

### Duties
- Assist with professional collections management including accession, deaccession, documentation, registration, and preparation
- Implement training, monitoring, and daily supervision of interns and volunteers
- Respond to internal and external queries and requests; facilitate use of collections
- Assist in maintaining collections metadata including digital records and multimedia files
- Implement and ensure adherence to all safety protocols
- Support and deliver internal and external outreach programs
- Assist with inventory, rehousing, organizing, and relocating collections
- Catalog and perform data entry into relational databases
- Photograph and document specimens
- Georeference specimen locality information
- Assist with processing incoming specimens and outgoing loans

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Bachelor's degree in relevant field
- At least 1 year museum collections management experience
- At least 1 year relational database experience
- Ability to handle biological material and museum specimens safely
- Intermediate skills with Microsoft Office Suite

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Master's degree in relevant field
- Experience with zoological or botanical museum specimens
- Understanding of taxonomic concepts and phylogenetic organization
- Experience moving and reorganizing collections
- Supervisory experience with interns and volunteers
- Advanced database experience including data imports and large dataset cleanup
- Experience engaging diverse audiences in science communication

### Physical Requirements
- Sitting and/or standing for up to 8 hours
- Handling objects up to 50 lbs occasionally, 30 lbs frequently
- Working with preservation chemicals

### Benefits
- Comprehensive benefits at larger institutions (medical, dental, vision)
- Paid time off for vacation, sickness, and holidays
- Retirement plans (403(b))
- Free/discounted museum membership
- Transit passes

## 5. Research Curator / Faculty Curator

### Equivalent Classifications
- GS-13 through GS-15 (federal equivalent, though most are in academic pay scales)
- "Assistant/Associate Curator" combined with "Assistant/Associate Professor" (Sam Noble, University of Oklahoma)
- "Staff Curator" (UC Berkeley MVZ)
- "Curator of [discipline]" (Santa Barbara Museum of Natural History, VA Museum of Natural History)

### Type of Employer
University museums with joint faculty appointments (Sam Noble/University of Oklahoma, UC Berkeley MVZ, Florida Museum of Natural History), freestanding museums with curatorial research programs (Santa Barbara Museum of Natural History, VA Museum of Natural History), and state museums.

### Salary
- **Range:** $48,000–$73,000+ (varies enormously by institution type)
- VA Museum of Natural History: $48,000–$50,000 (state pay band)
- Santa Barbara Museum of Natural History: $67,000–$73,000
- University of Oklahoma/Sam Noble: Competitive (not specified but tenure-track)
- UC Berkeley MVZ: Commensurate with experience
- University of Florida (Assistant Curator): Competitive and negotiable
- **Drivers of variation:** Whether position is tenure-track, institution prestige, geographic location, and research funding potential

### Experience
- **Research experience** beyond the PhD is typical for assistant curator positions
- Museum collections experience strongly preferred
- Teaching experience desired at university-affiliated positions

### Education
- **Required:** Ph.D. in relevant discipline (biology, zoology, geology, etc.)
- Research specialty in the collection's taxonomic focus area

---

### Description
Research Curators (often titled simply "Curator" at many institutions) are the scholarly leaders of natural history collections divisions. Unlike Collections Managers who focus on operational stewardship, Curators are primarily researchers who conduct specimen-based research, build collections through fieldwork, mentor graduate students, teach courses, and develop exhibitions. At university museums, these positions typically carry tenure-track or tenured faculty appointments with expectations for grant funding, publication, and graduate student mentorship. At freestanding museums, curators may have somewhat different balances of research versus public programming.

### Duties
- Conduct independent, specimen-based research program and publish in peer-reviewed journals
- Curate, grow, and develop the museum's collections through fieldwork and acquisitions
- Obtain external grant funding to support research and collections activities
- Teach and mentor undergraduate and graduate students
- Develop and contribute to museum exhibitions and public programs
- Provide taxonomic expertise and specimen identification services
- Serve as subject matter expert for the institution and the public
- Participate in professional societies and serve the broader disciplinary community
- Write grant proposals for collections improvement
- Collaborate with collections management staff on collection priorities
- Contribute to institutional strategic planning

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Ph.D. in relevant discipline
- Established record of high-quality research and publications
- Demonstrated record of or potential for strong extramural funding
- Specimen-based research experience
- Knowledge of relevant taxonomy and systematics
- Commitment to inclusive teaching and mentoring
- Excellent communication skills (written and oral)

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Postdoctoral experience
- Experience with museum collections management
- Exhibit development experience
- Knowledge of regional biodiversity relevant to the collection
- Experience working with underserved/underrepresented communities

### Physical Requirements
- Field work in variable conditions (heat, cold, humidity, rough terrain)
- Handling specimens of various sizes
- Standard laboratory and office environment work

### Benefits
- Tenure-track appointment (at university-affiliated positions)
- Start-up packages for research
- Access to graduate students and research infrastructure
- Sabbatical eligibility
- Comprehensive university benefits (health, retirement, tuition)
- Professional development and conference travel funding

## 6. Collections Informatics Manager / Database Manager

### Equivalent Classifications
- GS-11 through GS-13 (federal equivalent)
- "Database Manager for Collections" (AMNH), "Biodiversity Informatics Manager" (UC Berkeley), "Science Data Administrator" (DMNS, NC Museum), "Assistant Collections Information Manager" (Canadian Museum of Nature), "Head of Computer Systems" (Yale Peabody)

### Type of Employer
Large freestanding museums (AMNH, DMNS, Yale Peabody, Australian Museum), major university herbaria/museums (UC Berkeley), national/government museums (Canadian Museum of Nature, NC Museum of Natural Sciences), and international institutions. These positions exist at institutions with enough digital infrastructure to justify a dedicated informatics specialist.

### Salary
- **Range:** $55,464–$98,407/year USD
- UC Berkeley: $55,464–$71,712
- DMNS: $80,000–$93,000
- NC Museum: $65,604–$98,407 (state classification DT07)
- Canadian Museum of Nature: $60,929–$74,474 CAD (~$45,000–$55,000 USD)
- Australian Museum: $87,493–$96,540 AUD (~$57,000–$63,000 USD)
- Yale Peabody (Head of Computer Systems): Senior Manager grade (likely $80,000–$120,000+)
- **Drivers of variation:** Institution size, scope of responsibility (single-collection vs. enterprise-wide), and geographic location

### Experience
- **Minimum:** 2 years relevant experience in collections management and/or database administration
- **Senior positions:** 3–5+ years of experience with museum collections data and digitization projects
- Systems administration experience valued

### Education
- **Range:** Bachelor's degree in computer science, information technology, natural sciences, or library science
- Master's degree preferred for senior positions
- Computer science or IT background combined with natural history knowledge is the ideal profile

---

### Description
The Collections Informatics Manager is responsible for the technical management and strategic development of an institution's digital collections infrastructure. This role bridges the gap between collections staff (who generate and use data) and IT departments (who maintain hardware and networks). The informatics manager administers collections management systems (EMu, Specify, Arctos, Symbiota, etc.), ensures data quality and standards compliance, develops custom reports and data tools, manages data publication to external aggregators, and provides training and user support. At larger institutions, this may be an enterprise-wide role spanning multiple collections; at smaller ones, it may be combined with other duties.

### Duties
- Administer and maintain collections management systems (EMu, Specify, Arctos, Symbiota, CollectionSpace, etc.)
- Liaise with external software vendors on maintenance, upgrades, and new features
- Ensure security, integrity, and accessibility of database systems
- Implement institutional and disciplinary data standards
- Develop custom data imports, exports, reports, and queries
- Manage data publication to aggregators (GBIF, iDigBio, SCAN, etc.)
- Provide training and user support for collections staff
- Lead or support digitization projects and workflows
- Manage digital asset management systems
- Troubleshoot database and application performance issues
- Develop and maintain web-based collections access portals
- Write and contribute to grant proposals for collections digitization and informatics
- Serve as liaison between collections staff and institutional IT departments
- Monitor and evaluate database applications; plan for long-term data management
- Maintain hardware and software infrastructure
- Configure and customize database systems to meet institutional needs

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Bachelor's degree in IT, computer science, natural sciences, or library/information science
- Proficiency with SQL and relational database management
- Experience with collections management software (EMu, Specify, Arctos, or similar)
- Knowledge of biodiversity data standards (Darwin Core, EML, etc.)
- Strong communication skills and ability to work with diverse user groups
- Problem-solving and troubleshooting abilities
- Familiarity with multiple operating systems (Linux, macOS, Windows)

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Advanced degree in relevant field
- Experience with digital asset management systems
- Familiarity with data aggregators (GBIF, iDigBio)
- Programming skills (Python, PHP, JavaScript, R)
- Experience with web development (HTML, CSS)
- Knowledge of API development and implementation
- Crystal Reports or similar reporting tool experience
- Previous experience with digitization protocols and best practices

### Physical Requirements
- Primarily sedentary desk work
- Some physical requirements may apply when working directly with collections

### Benefits
- Comprehensive benefits at most institutions
- Hybrid/remote work options increasingly available
- Professional development opportunities
- Access to university or museum resources

## 7. Biodiversity Informatics Software Developer / Engineer

### Equivalent Classifications
- No standard civil service equivalent; university IT classifications vary
- "Biodiversity Informatician" (ASU), "Bioinformatics Software Engineer" (University of Kansas/Specify), "Software Engineer" (ASU/BioKIC), "Biodiversity Informatics Software Developer" (Yale Peabody)

### Type of Employer
Universities hosting major biodiversity informatics platforms (University of Kansas/Specify, ASU/Symbiota/BioKIC), large natural history museums (Yale Peabody), and collaborative multi-institutional projects (iDigBio). These positions support the development of software tools used by the broader collections community.

### Salary
- **Range:** $30,000–$80,000/year USD
- ASU part-time: $30,000–$36,000
- ASU full-time: $55,440–$80,000
- University of Kansas/Specify: $70,000–$80,000
- Yale Peabody: Not specified (likely $60,000–$90,000 based on grade)
- **Drivers of variation:** Full-time vs. part-time, experience level, geographic location

### Experience
- **Minimum:** 2 years of professional software development experience (not coursework)
- **Senior positions:** 4–5+ years including database programming and web application development
- Experience with biodiversity-specific tools valued but not always required

### Education
- **Minimum:** Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Information Technology, or related field (or equivalent combination of education and experience)
- Graduate degree in biology or computer science preferred for senior roles

---

### Description
Biodiversity Informatics Software Developers build and maintain the software infrastructure that enables natural history collections to be digitized, managed, and shared with the world. These positions focus on writing code for platforms like Specify, Symbiota, and custom institutional tools. The work spans front-end web development, back-end database programming, API development, and systems administration. Unlike informatics managers who focus on using existing tools, developers create and improve the tools themselves. The field is characterized by a small, close-knit community of practitioners working on open-source projects with global impact.

### Duties
- Design, implement, debug, and maintain biodiversity software applications
- Develop intuitive user interfaces and great user experiences for researchers
- Participate in software design and requirements gathering with stakeholders
- Develop and maintain relational database systems (MySQL, MariaDB, PostgreSQL)
- Build web services, APIs, and data integration pipelines
- Contribute to open-source code bases (Symbiota, Specify, etc.)
- Coordinate with portal data managers and user community managers
- Perform server management duties including security, updates, and optimization
- Write documentation and training materials
- Participate in code reviews, stand-up meetings, and team design sessions
- Test new software releases and evaluate user-requested features
- Facilitate biodiversity data integration with global aggregators (GBIF, iDigBio)

### Required Skills and Qualifications
- Proficiency in programming languages (JavaScript, Python, PHP, Java, TypeScript)
- SQL proficiency and relational database design principles
- Understanding of web development standards and DevOps technologies
- Experience with version control (Git/GitHub)
- Strong written and verbal communication skills
- Ability to work independently and collaboratively

### Desired Skills and Qualifications
- Knowledge of biodiversity data standards (Darwin Core)
- Experience with biodiversity-specific software platforms (Symbiota, Specify)
- Front-end JavaScript frameworks (React.js or similar)
- Linux system administration
- Docker/Kubernetes and containerization technologies
- Experience with test automation and CI/CD
- Background in museum or environmental science data

### Physical Requirements
- Primarily sedentary office/computer work
- May require occasional travel to conferences or user sites

### Benefits
- University employee benefits packages
- Flexible and hybrid/remote work options commonly available
- Professional growth opportunities
- Pleasant work environment emphasized by Specify Consortium postings

## 8. Digitization Technician / Specialist

### Equivalent Classifications
- No standard civil service equivalent
- "Curatorial Assistant II - Digitization" (Cal Academy), "Digitization Technician" (California Botanic Garden), "Imaging and Georeferencing Technician" (AMNH), "Digital Imaging Specialist" (NHMLA), "Specimen Digitizer Intern" (California Botanic Garden)

### Type of Employer
Found across institution types but most frequently at botanical gardens and herbaria (California Botanic Garden, Missouri Botanical Garden), large museums (AMNH, NHMLA, California Academy of Sciences), and project-funded positions at various institutions. Most positions are temporary/grant-funded, reflecting the project-based nature of large-scale digitization efforts.

### Salary

## Cross-Cutting Observations

### Salary Compression and Career Economics
The data reveals significant salary compression in the natural history collections field. A Collections Manager at a small university museum (Angelo State, $34,611) earns less than an entry-level Museum Specialist at AMNH ($42,970–$59,946). PhD-level Curatorial Associates at AMNH ($80,000–$98,000) earn modestly more than experienced Collections Managers at mid-tier institutions ($58,000–$78,000). The most dramatic salary disparities exist between comparable positions at different institution types — the same functional role can vary by $30,000–$40,000 depending on the employer.

### The Grant-Funding Problem
A substantial proportion of positions in this field are grant-funded and term-limited, particularly in digitization, informatics, and assistant collections manager roles. This creates significant career instability, with many professionals cycling through 2–3 year positions at different institutions. Only senior collections manager positions and faculty curator positions are consistently permanent.

### The Informatics Imperative
Biodiversity informatics skills are increasingly required across all position types, not just dedicated informatics roles. Familiarity with databases, data standards (Darwin Core), aggregators (GBIF, iDigBio), and digital imaging is now expected even at entry level. Institutions are recognizing this by creating dedicated informatics positions, but the demand for these skills far outstrips the supply of qualified candidates.

### Employer Type Matters
- **Large freestanding museums** (AMNH, Field Museum, California Academy of Sciences, NHMLA): Best staffing ratios, most specialized positions, generally good benefits, but high cost-of-living locations
- **R1 university museums** (University of Michigan, University of Florida, Cornell): Strong benefits through university systems, research opportunities, but positions often report to academic departments with competing priorities
- **Smaller university museums** (Angelo State, Auburn, Idaho State): Lower salaries but broader scope; collections managers may oversee multiple collection types and gain diverse experience
- **State government museums** (NC Museum, VA Museum, Royal BC Museum): Civil service classifications with defined pay bands; stable employment but lower salary ceilings; good benefits
- **Botanical gardens** (California Botanic Garden, Missouri Botanical Garden, NYBG): Variable; large gardens rival museums in complexity while smaller gardens offer part-time opportunities
- **Federal (Smithsonian)**: GS-13 positions at $121,785–$158,322 represent the highest-paying collections management positions in the field, with excellent federal benefits
